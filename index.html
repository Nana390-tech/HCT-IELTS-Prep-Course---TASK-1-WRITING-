The Big Picture

What is Academic Task 1?

You are given one visual and must describe it in your own words in about 20 minutes. It is a factual report — you describe what you see, you never give opinions or reasons.

The 3 Essential Patterns

Say the same idea 3 ways

Pattern 1: There + was + a + adjective + noun + in + the…
There was a steady rise in the price of oil in 2015.
Pattern 2: Noun + verb + adverb
The price of oil rose steadily in 2015.
Pattern 3: Time + saw / witnessed + adjective + noun + in…
2015 saw a steady rise in the price of oil.  (no "in" before the time!)

Sentence 2

The Overview = the big picture, no numbers

Step back and describe the visual like a picture. What is the 1–2 main trend? Use no exact figures here.

Start with: Overall, it can be seen that… · It is noticeable that… · In general,…

Example overview (my own wording)

"Overall, sales rose in all three regions over the period, with the North recording the most striking increase." — notice: a clear trend, a comparison, and no precise numbers.

💡 Tip: write the overview after the introduction. If you run out of time and skip it, you cannot reach Band 6.

Quick Guide

Which language for which visual?

First ask: Is there a time frame? If yes → language of change. If no → comparison only.

VisualTime?LanguageKey tip
📈 LineAlwaysChange + compareGroup lines by similar pattern
📊 BarSometimesCompare (change if dated)Check for dates first!
🥧 PieSometimes%, fractions, proportionsStart with the biggest slice
🔢 TableSometimesSame as line/barScan for high/low patterns
⚙️ ProcessNoPassive + time linkersDescribe every stage in order
🗺️ MapUsuallyLocation + changeGroup by area or by feature
🥧 Pie Charts

Fraction ↔ Percentage

Pie charts love variety: swap between %, fractions and qualifiers like "just over" / "nearly".

⚙️ Process Diagrams

Use the passive voice

In a process we care about what happens, not who does it → present simple passive.

Active: Someone collects the beans.
Passive: The beans are collected. ✅

Time linkers to join your stages

First · Next · After that · Following this · Then · Subsequently · Finally
Complex: Once the milk has been heated… · After being packed, the goods are shipped. · Having been dried, the leaves are sorted.

Quick check: make it passive

🗺️ Maps

Language of location

Positionin front of · opposite · next to · beside · behind · between · alongside · to the left/right of
Compassin the north · to the east · in the south-west · on the west side · from west to east

Language of change for maps

An airport was built · the houses were replaced by apartments · the forest was cleared to make way for a factory · the road was extended.

💡 Group your map answer by area (north vs south) or by feature (transport, housing, business) — whichever is clearer.
